<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Some Sundays move at their own slow pace, and yesterday was one of them.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I went into the weekend feeling excited to refactor a piece of code that’s been bothering me for weeks. But by Sunday evening, the work was still unfinished and instead of accepting that, I spent way too much time complaining to myself about everything that didn’t go according to plan.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It’s one of my worst habits. The moment a day starts slipping off track, I start overanalyzing everything before I’ve even done anything to fix it. I turn frustration into a full internal debate, which never actually helps.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Now Monday is right around the corner. The first <em>real</em> week of the new year. The teams, clients, and projects are all ramping up. The calm of the holidays is over, and the hustle is coming back.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For me, balancing long-term improvements with immediate responsibilities is one of the hardest parts of running a company. Some days it feels less like progress and more like choosing between competing priorities.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">But if today reminded me of anything, it’s this: <strong>complaining doesn’t ship anything. Growth doesn’t happen through frustration</strong>. Tt happens through action. The best way to move forward is to stop dwelling on what’s lost and focus on what can still be built.</p>
