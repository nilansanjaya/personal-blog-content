<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong><em>Disclaimer: </em></strong><em>This isn’t about the projects and tasks we handle as a company — these are my personal strategies for managing the varied responsibilities of being a founder. Our clients are happy and have been with us for years, thanks to the great work our team delivers.</em></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In my journey as a founder, I’ve come across one recurring challenge that resonates across most people I meet in the community: managing time, responsibilities, and the constant demand to shift between different “hats” with unique priorities.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This isn’t just about fitting tasks into a calendar or time boxing each task. Founders are constantly challenged with various responsibilities — from securing new clients to refining product strategies and staying hands-on with their teams.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Here’s how I’ve approached balancing my work, life, and the methods I’ve found to be working for me along the way.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>A little bit of background about what I do as a founder,</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Right now my responsibilities are distributed across few areas.</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Client Relations:</strong> I’m responsible for finding new clients, pitching, sending proposals, and ensuring that our existing clients remain happy and satisfied.</li>



<li><strong>Product Development:</strong> I strategise product development for both internal and client projects, planning development resources, technical architecture, and infrastructure requirements to meet the goals.</li>



<li><strong>Hands-on Team Engagement:</strong> I work closely with all my teams, stay involved in coding, reviewing PRs, and daily technical discussions. When needed, I jump in to code or fix issues myself.</li>



<li><strong>Uncategorised:</strong> Probably everything else.</li>
</ol>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It’s a lot to juggle (So I’ve been frequently told), and while I’ve tried various apps and productivity tools, I realised the solution wasn’t in the tool — it was in finding a system of my own that matched my work style and responsibilities.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>The never-ending-to-do list</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">One of the toughest aspects has been managing a never-ending backlog of tasks. For every item I cross off, more seems to appear, especially when balancing my personal life, family, friends, and self-care. Finding a way to work that didn’t overwhelm me became critical, and after a lot of trial and error, I’ve landed on a few methods that have made a huge difference for me.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>How I’m tackling priorities and tasks</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>1) I keep two separate backlogs</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A <strong>time-sensitive </strong>backlog, covering tasks that have strict deadlines or need quick responses. A non-time-sensitive backlog, which contains tasks linked to different areas of responsibility but without urgent time constraints.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A <strong>non-time-sensitive</strong> backlog, which contains tasks linked to different areas of responsibility but without urgent time constraints.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This separation helps me maintain focus on what’s immediate while also making steady progress on the important goals.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>2) Right task in the right tool</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">My time-sensitive backlog is in an app that has my task list top-to-bottom right next to a calendar, helping me manage date-times and deadlines efficiently. The other backlog sits in a kanban-style board, where I track tasks by their status or stage — perfect for me to see where my things are at.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>3) Right task at the right place</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This method has been a huge life changer. I adjust my tasks based on my environment. If I’m at a family gathering or travelling, I tackle tasks that require less concentration. For high-focus work (that isn’t coding), I change settings entirely. Heading to a co-working space or a quiet café helps me fully focus, often working on one task “window” at a time. This shift in setting has proven invaluable for maintaining clarity and creativity — removing distractions and focusing on complex problems without interruption when i need to think.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Some bonus habits that have been immensely helpful to me</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Travel, yes travel!</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Traveling regularly gives me a much-needed reset. Stepping away from the day-to-day routine helps me return with a fresh mindset and often inspires new ideas or solutions that I’d never have considered while I’m in the usual workspace.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Meditation</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Meditation has become a daily habit for me that clears my mind and builds focus. I’ve gotten completely addicted to it. Whether it’s just a few minutes in the morning or a midday reset, meditation has a huge impact on how I handle everyday ups and downs. I would highly recommend everyone to try this.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Navigating the demands of a founder’s role can feel relentless, but these practices have helped me find a much more balanced and clarity in my day-to-day work so far. To fellow founders facing similar challenges: sometimes the key is finding a structure that truly supports your unique responsibilities and work style.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Would love to hear your thoughts, and how you handle your day-to-day backlogs and responsibilities.</p>
