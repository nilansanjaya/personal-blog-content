<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As most people were winding down from Christmas Eve to the 28th, I did something a little different.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I gave my team the week off, told our clients we were on holiday break, and then quietly disappeared into our codebase.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Running a 24/7 ops company means “off” time is rare. There are always pings, messages, fires, and decisions waiting. This was my first real five-day window in a long time with no calls, no Slack noise, and almost no social media. Sleep? Pretty optional.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Why I chose to work on&nbsp;holiday</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">On paper, it sounds strange: everyone is on vacation, and the founder is working.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">But what I wanted from this break wasn’t rest, it was <strong>focus</strong>. The kind that is almost impossible to get when you’re context switching all day.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I’ve had a list of technical things I wanted to do for months:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Clean up tech debt that we’ve been working around</li>



<li>Refactor parts of the system that only get quick fixes during busy weeks</li>



<li>Improve some “invisible” pieces of infrastructure that matter a lot but never feel urgent</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This week finally gave me permission to go heads down.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">What I actually got&nbsp;done</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">With no one needing me in real time, the days looked simple: wake up, code, walk, code, repeat.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Across those five days, I managed to:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Jump back into the code and fix bugs that usually hide in the daily hustle</li>



<li>Clear out old tech mess and simplify pieces that were getting too complex</li>



<li>Make core parts of our system faster, safer, and easier to work on for the team</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It didn’t feel like a grind. It felt like finally scratching an itch that had been there for months.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Why this kind of deep work&nbsp;matters</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">These quiet days won’t show up directly on a marketing dashboard.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">But they will show up in fewer incidents, faster shipping, and a team that can move more confidently when everyone is back.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For me as a founder, it was also a reset.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Stepping back into the code reminded me why I started building in the first place and gave me a clearer sense of where we should take the product next.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I’m not done yet, but this short “deep work retreat” over Christmas was a total win.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Over to&nbsp;you</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If you’re a founder, builder, or maker:</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When was the last time you had real, uninterrupted time to build?</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">And how do you protect that kind of deep work in your own schedule?</p>
